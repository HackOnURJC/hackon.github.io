"(Broken) Promises of Privacy"

RESPONSABLE

Claudio Soriente

Privacy of your online activity relies on a number of complicated protocols and, ultimately, on encryption. You might have heard CEOs of digital companies bragging about the security of their customers because their data is protected by top-notch, state-of-the-art or even military-grade encryption. It is as if encryption was the one silver bullet for all privacy offenders. In this talk I will give a few examples where you were promised privacy, but then the promise was broken.
